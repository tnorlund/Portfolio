4. [med] `infra/email_receipt_inbox/infrastructure.py:159-174` — Direct asynchronous S3 invocation has no failed-event destination, DLQ, or durable backpressure. After Lambda exhausts its retry window, events can be discarded, particularly during public-ingress bursts. Configure an on-failure destination and alarms, or preferably place SQS plus a DLQ between S3 and Lambda. [Lambda defaults to two retries and discards exhausted events without a destination](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/lambda/latest/dg/invocation-async-error-handling.html).

[med] `infra/email_receipt_inbox/infrastructure.py:76-80` and `lambdas/handler.py:66-97` — Replay lineage is not reliable: the bucket is unversioned, the handler fetches the latest object rather than the event’s version, ignores `sequencer`/ETag, and downstream identity uses the sender-controlled RFC `Message-ID`. [S3 notifications are duplicated and unordered](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonS3/latest/userguide/notification-how-to-event-types-and-destinations.html).
